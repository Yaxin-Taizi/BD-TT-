Key Findings at a Glance

  • Strength Career growth opportunities are our strongest brand association, ranking #1 for ByteDance and top 3 for TikTok across all segments.
  • Strength Competitive salary, learning & development, and "good reference for future career" are sweet spots where candidate priorities align with our brand perception.
  • Gap Job security and work-life balance are top 3 candidate priorities but among the attributes least associated with us. Both brands trail Google, Meta, and Amazon.
  • Gap 35% of non-considerers cite "I don't know enough about the company" for ByteDance; work-life balance concerns top TikTok barriers at 32%.
  • Opportunity Funnel drop-off is significant: awareness is strong (82-92% for ByteDance in key markets, 90%+ for TikTok), but potential applicants are only 8% (BD) and 12% (TT).
  • Channel LinkedIn (65%), online job boards (55%), and career websites (54%) are the top discovery channels. Experienced hires trust employee conversations most (51%).

6How Candidates Get to Know Us

Top Channels Used

  1. LinkedIn (65%)
    "LinkedIn is where I see things first, but I only click if it feels specific."
  2. Online job boards (55%)
    Consistent across all segments and markets.
  3. Company career websites (54%)
    Used for fast answers: what teams do, how work is structured.

Japan is unique: Company website (68%), Online job boards (62%), Snapchat (50%)

Top Sources Influencing Impressions

  1. Company website (48%)
    But candidates consume with discretion — "what they describe could be just some kind of fluffy."
  2. Online employee reviews (43%)
    Glassdoor, Blind. "I use it more to see if the same issues keep coming up."
  3. Conversations with current/former employees (42%)
    For experienced hires (51%), this is THE most trusted source. "That's the most reliable signal for me."

Top Content Preferences

  1. Short-form videos & day-in-the-life stories (50%)
    "Informative promotional video that doesn't only show the building but also the technology and the day-to-day life."
  2. Project or case study highlights (36%)
    Technical blogs, post-mortems and project deep-dives signal seriousness.
  3. Blog posts or articles by employees (36%)
    "I trust content more when it comes from actual engineers talking about challenges or trade-offs."

Key insight: Content from engineers themselves carries far more weight than centrally produced employer stories. "If it looks like marketing, I usually skip it."

Addressing Negative Conceptions

Job Security & Business Stability

#3 candidate priority (59%), but one of the attributes least associated with us. ByteDance trails Amazon, Meta, and Google. TikTok concern at 28%: "Concerns about job or business stability."

Action: Build trust by proactively sharing content on business direction, stability, and long-term strategy in a transparent manner.

Work-Life Balance

Top barrier for TikTok (32%) and ByteDance SG (35%). Candidates define it as reasonable working hours (53%) and growing career without sacrificing personal life (47%).

Action: Provide transparent, credible content on what work-life balance looks like, using real examples and employee perspectives. Not polished — real.

"Don't Know Enough About the Company"

35% of ByteDance non-considerers. Awareness is high but understanding is shallow. Candidates don't know what it's like to work here day to day.

Action: Improve understanding through clearer, more authentic storytelling through the lens of employees. Better alignment of EVP pillars based on candidate insights.

Role Clarity & Job Fit

28% cite "No job opportunities that fit my profile." Focus groups reveal cluttered, vague job descriptions and inconsistent information during interviews.

Action: Provide clear, detailed, consistent information about roles, teams and responsibilities to reduce uncertainty and make it easier for the right talent to engage.

2026 Priorities

  • 1 Strengthen work-life balance narrative — Provide transparent, credible content on what WLB looks like for us, using real employee examples. Not polished brand messaging — "if it looks like marketing, I usually skip it."
  • 2 Build job stability confidence — Proactively share content on business direction, financial health, and long-term strategy. Candidates equate stability with "clear and sustainable revenue" and "low layoff rates."
  • 3 Close the knowledge gap — Convert awareness into understanding. Focus on authentic employee storytelling, day-in-the-life content, and technical deep-dives that show what we actually do, not who we say we are.
  • 4 Improve role clarity and JD quality — Reduce cluttered, vague job descriptions. Provide clear team context, specific responsibilities, and consistent information throughout the hiring process.
  • 5 Activate employees as advocates — For experienced hires (3+ yrs), conversations with current/former employees are the #1 trusted source (51%). Strengthen how we bring our EVP to life internally to turn employees into authentic advocates.
